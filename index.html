Neurodivergency

What is Neurodivergency?

“Neurodivergency” is an umbrella term referring to a wide variety of learning and developmental disorders. Two of the most well-known ones are Autism Spectrum Disorder and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder also known as ASD and ADHD. However the term also includes Tourette’s Syndrome, Dyslexia, Dyspraxia, Dyscalculia, Dysgaphia, Meares-Irlen Syndrome, Hyperlexia and Synaesthesia. In comparison to how much of the population is affected, since between 30% and 40% of the total population is thought to be neurodiverse, there is astonishingly little cultural and social awareness of how they experience the world, how they are generally treated by society and how to accommodate non neurotypical people. This in my personal experience of listening to people (I am no psychologist) with any of the conditions that the term refers to, is that they were in some way shape or form mistreated by society at some point in their life.
